Disaster Management FAQs

What should I do before a disaster strikes?
Prepare an emergency kit, identify safe areas, plan evacuation routes, and stay updated with official alerts.
How do I register as a volunteer?
You can register on our Volunteer Registration page and start contributing.
What emergency contact numbers should I know?
For disaster-related emergencies, dial 1070 or contact your local disaster relief center.
How can I donate to disaster relief efforts?
Visit the Donation Page to contribute to relief efforts.
Where can I find real-time disaster alerts?
Latest disaster alerts are displayed on the homepage under the 'Disaster Alerts' section.
How do I report a disaster in my area?
You can report a disaster by calling your local emergency hotline or using disaster management apps like NDMA or state-specific apps.
How can I locate a missing person after a disaster?
You can check with local authorities, relief organizations, and online missing person registries like Google Person Finder.
How are relief camps organized?
Relief camps are set up by government and NGOs, providing food, shelter, and medical aid to disaster-affected people.
What should I carry to a relief camp?
Carry basic essentials such as ID documents, clothing, medications, food, water, and hygiene items.
How can I check if my area is disaster-prone?
Refer to government disaster maps, local emergency services, and historical data on past disasters in your area.
How can I volunteer at a relief camp?
After registering as a volunteer, check available relief camps and sign up to assist in food distribution, medical aid, or logistics.
